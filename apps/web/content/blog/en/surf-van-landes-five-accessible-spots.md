# Surf and van life in the Landes: five accessible spots for a two or three-day trip

A short surf trip in the Landes is above all a question of tide and parking. You leave Capbreton on a Thursday afternoon, come back Sunday evening, you have surfed four or five sessions, you have slept three metres from the board. That is the format we unpack here.

Five spots selected for their accessibility by camper van, their off-season overnight tolerance, and the quality of their waves. From Capbreton to Mimizan, two hours of driving at most all told. Tested with Marceau, the workshop's Ford Transit Custom with a roof tent, compact format, perfect for slipping onto beach car parks.

The Landes remain a due-west-facing coast. Westerly and north-westerly swells dominate, and most spots break on sand bottoms, which makes them accessible to nearly every level, depending on size and wind.

## 1. La Piste, Capbreton — intermediate and above

La Piste beach, south of Capbreton, is the town's historic spot. A big powerful wave when the swell is solid, more manageable in summer. West-north-west orientation, works with an easterly (offshore) wind.

Recommended level: intermediate to expert. The shore break is dry, not for beginners. Best time: incoming tide, two hours before high water.

Tarmac car park along the dune, overnight van parking tolerated midweek out of season. Board and wetsuit hire at Hossegor Surf Shop, rue des Tisserands, or at Rainbow Surf Shop in Capbreton. After the session, head for La Cantina in Capbreton, quick south-western cooking, tapas and bavette steak, count €20.

Tip: park at an angle, leave the exits clear, the fire service drive through regularly.

## 2. Les Estagnots, Seignosse — all levels

Les Estagnots is the archetypal learner beach of the Landes coast. Sand bottom, regular waves, few currents on the first two sandbars. Surf schools follow one another from morning to night.

Recommended level: beginner to intermediate. Best time: low to mid-tide, the waves are more spaced out and longer. Offshore (easterly) wind until about 10 am generally.

Large car park, paid in July and August, free out of season. Overnight stays tolerated at the shoulders of the season. Hire at Hossegor Surf Club, right on the sand, €15 for a wetsuit plus board for the day. For lunch, Le Shack at Estagnots beach serves decent poke bowls and bao sandwiches, twenty-minute wait in July.

Tip: arrive at 8 am in summer, you will have the sandbar to yourself for an hour.

## 3. Les Bourdaines, Seignosse — intermediate

Five hundred metres north of Les Estagnots, Les Bourdaines offers a hollower, faster spot for surfers who have outgrown the school level but are not quite ready for La Piste.

West-facing, works well with a south-westerly swell. Best time: incoming tide, easterly or no wind. Recommended level: confirmed intermediate.

Same car park as Les Estagnots, then walk north along the beach. No hire on site, kit up in Hossegor or central Seignosse. After the session, Le Hagoa in Seignosse, beside the lake, offers a short menu and a good fish of the day for around €25.

Tip: take a long leash, the lateral current pushes quickly to the south.

## 4. Le Penon, Seignosse — all levels

Le Penon remains one of the most versatile spots on the coast. Several aligned sandbars, peaks for every level, a length of beach that absorbs busy days.

West-facing. Best time: mid-incoming tide. Wind: offshore in the morning, westerly in the afternoon which flattens the wave.

Van-friendly car park in front of the motorhome aire, services a hundred metres away. Hire at Pukas Surf School, well established on the seafront. For dinner, Le Penon restaurant, facing the beach, for a catch of the day on the plancha and a glass of white Tursan. Budget €30 with wine.

Tip: the evening session in late September is the most beautiful. Low raking light, water still at 18 degrees.

## 5. Mimizan-Plage sud — intermediate to expert

Further north, Mimizan-Plage sud offers hollower, less crowded waves than the southern Landes. The spot works on every tide, with a peak of activity on a falling tide.

Recommended level: intermediate and above, the shore break can be violent on swell days. West-facing orientation, easterly wind ideal.

Parking on the south beach, tolerated overnight out of season. Hire at Mimizan Surf Club on the seafront, a local crew who know every sandbar. After the session, head for La Plancha at Mimizan-Plage, wooden terrace, plate of squid and grilled red peppers, €20 per person.

Tip: Mimizan is less crowded, but the CRS lifeguards close bathing as soon as the red flag goes up. Respect it, the Courant de Sainte-Eulalie is treacherous.

## Reading the sea and the forecast

Useful apps: Surf Report for hourly forecasts, Windy for wind, Windguru for swell. The ideal Landes wave: swell of 1.2 to 1.8 metres, period above 10 seconds, easterly or no wind. Above 2.5 metres, the spots get heavy, leave them to the confirmed.

Tides: in the Landes, the range is large. A tide can transform a sandbar in two hours. Note the high water time at your spot, slot your session into the window that precedes it by two hours.

## Mini checklist: what to pack in the van for a short surf trip

For two people, three days:

- Boards: two, plus a spare if there is room
- Wetsuits: a 3/2 mm for summer, a 4/3 mm for the shoulder season
- Leashes and wax bars suited to the water temperature
- Poncho or hooded towel, two per person
- Five-litre container of fresh water for rinsing kit
- Separate jerry can of drinking water
- Factor 50 sunscreen, even in overcast weather
- Waterproof bags for wet wetsuits
- A protective board for the rear floor, to lay soaked wetsuits on
- Knife, cloth, wax scraper
- Basic first aid: strips, disinfectant, anti-inflammatory, plasters for heels
- A paper guide to the spots, more reliable than the network on the beachfront

Leave at home: city shoes, two pairs of flip-flops are enough.
