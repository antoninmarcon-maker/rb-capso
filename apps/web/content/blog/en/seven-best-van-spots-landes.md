# The seven finest van spots in the Landes

The Landes often get driven through too quickly. You race towards the Basque Country, skip past Mimizan, miss Contis altogether. That is a shame. Between the pine forest whistling in the west wind and the beaches that change face every six hours, there is enough here to park the van for several nights without ever seeing the same sky twice.

Here are seven van spots in the Landes, scouted over years of assignments and tested with Marceau and Lazare, the two vans from the RB-CapSO workshop in Capbreton. Official aires, tolerated car parks, more confidential corners. For each stop, the useful information, the feel of the place, and the small detail you will not read elsewhere.

A firm reminder: in France, wild camping in a van is not banned on principle, but overnight parking is governed by each local council. Respect the signs, empty your grey water in the designated aires, and do not put out table or awning if you are on a public car park. That is often what tips the balance between tolerance and a fine.

## 1. Capbreton, the Pêcherie aire

The natural starting point when you leave the workshop. Capbreton's municipal motorhome aire, on the fishing port side, welcomes camper vans for around €10 a night. Water services, waste disposal, electric hook-ups. Nothing bucolic about it, but you sleep, you wash, you hit the road with a clear head.

The bonus: you are five minutes on foot from the covered market and the morning stalls. Drop into La Poissonnerie du Port for a slice of tuna belly or a couple of Arcachon oysters to eat on a bench. The smell of iodine is sharp, a little acrid at daybreak when the boats come in.

Tip: avoid weekends in August, full by 2 pm.

## 2. Hossegor, Culs Nus beach car park

Between Hossegor and Seignosse, the Culs Nus beach car park is still tolerated for a night in the van, out of season. In July and August, the local police do the rounds and move people on. From April to June and from September to November, it is peaceful.

The ground is sandy, the pine forest begins a couple of steps away. At dawn, you step over the dune and have the Atlantic to yourself, the surfers and a handful of pensioners in full-body swimsuits. The easterly wind, on September mornings, leaves the sea as smooth as a varnished board.

Tip: park in reverse, facing the exit. Sandy tracks close up quickly after a shower.

## 3. Seignosse, Penon motorhome aire

Simple municipal aire, shaded, beneath the maritime pines. Around €10 a night, 48 hours maximum, full services. You can hear the waves even with the doors closed, a continuous rumble that rocks you better than any lullaby.

Ten minutes on foot is enough to reach the Penon surf spot. In the evening, pop into Ziggy Café du Penon for a draught beer and grilled sardines. Family atmosphere, noisy, inexpensive.

Tip: hook up on the northern side of the aire, the pitches are wider for a Ducato like Lazare.

## 4. Messanges, central beach car park

Overnight tolerance car park, free out of season. Concrete surface, a few street lamps, no service points. It is stripped back, but you sleep well. Sea spray settles on the windscreen, you will need to wipe it off in the morning.

Messanges is a straight, long, no-nonsense beach. The pure Landes coast. For breakfast, walk up to the beach hut for a coffee and a slice of gâteau basque. On October evenings, the sky turns blood orange behind the dune.

Tip: bring a folding chair, nothing to hire on site.

## 5. Vieux-Boucau, lake aire

Parking area beside the Port d'Albret lake. A rare setting in the region: calm water, pine forest behind, and the sound of the ocean arriving muffled through the dune. €9 a night, 72 hours permitted.

You can walk around the lake in an hour, or hire a paddleboard at the watersports base. For food, La Cabane du Lac serves unpretentious moules-frites, wooden terrace, view over the water. The smell of warm resin hangs in the air on July afternoons.

Tip: watch out for mosquitoes at dusk, close the screens before you pour the aperitif.

## 6. Contis-Plage, beach car park

Further north, Contis remains one of the last spots tolerated without heavy infrastructure. Packed sand car park, tall pines, a lighthouse blinking in the distance. No services, overnight parking tacitly accepted out of season.

It is confidential, silent, and local surfers gather here for long sessions. At the bar-restaurant Le Quai, opposite the lighthouse, you can have a plate of plancha squid for around €15.

Tip: bring water in a jerry can, the nearest water point is in Saint-Julien-en-Born.

## 7. Mimizan, Courant aire

Motorhome aire at the Courant de Sainte-Eulalie, beside the freshwater stream that winds its way to the ocean. Wooded setting, full services, around €12 a night. Very popular in summer, quiet from mid-September.

In the morning, walk down to the mouth. Fresh water meets the Atlantic in a churn that leaves shells in a perfect arc on the sand. For lunch, the crêperie Chez Maïté at Mimizan-Plage serves decent buckwheat galettes and a Landes cider you will not find elsewhere.

Tip: mind the current, supervised bathing is only on the south beach.

## Preparing the route without nasty surprises

Fill your jerry cans before heading north, water points thin out above Mimizan. Check the weather: a strong westerly makes the beachfront car parks noisy, even with the doors shut. Bring a board for soft sand, a cloth for the sea spray, and above all time. The Landes coast is not something you visit, it is something you wait for.
